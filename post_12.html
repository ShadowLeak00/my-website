April 16, 2025

Finding the right tools for .onion exploration is key in any OSINT investigation. Below are the top Tor search engines that can significantly expand your reach into the hidden web.

1. Ahmia (ahmia.fi)

One of the most widely used Tor search engines. It filters illegal content and presents indexed results cleanly. Great as a starting point.

2. Dark Search (darksearch.io)

Launched in 2019, this engine delivers richer results than Ahmia. Especially useful for searching emails or specific keywords like "OSINT".

3. Onion Link (onion.link)

Powered by Google Custom Search, this tool appends .link to .onion domains, letting you access dark web pages via clearnet proxies.

4. Tor Search Engine (torsearchengine.net)

Uses Google indexing of Tor URLs via proxies. A more traditional interface for those familiar with Google syntax.

5. Onionland Search (onionlandsearchengine.com)

Another Google-backed CSE tool for searching the dark web through proxy links. Results quality can vary but still worth trying.

6. Tor2Web (tor2web.org / onion.ly)

This isn't a search engine itself, but a method to access .onion links from your browser. Just swap .onion with .onion.ly or use tor2web.org proxies.

7. Not Evil (onion only: hss3uro2hsxfogfq.onion)

A Tor-native engine with a minimal interface. Can be effective but availability and reliability fluctuate.
